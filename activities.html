Activities

Our framework for applied learning and collaborative engagement to drive real-world policy impact.

Learning Model

How will members learn? Active, peer-driven and practical methods facilitate the learning process of our CoP members.

  • Peer-led learning sessions and discussions: Organized and facilitated by emerging practitioners, these sessions feature:
    • Topic presentations relevant to real-world challenges
    • Group discussions and collaborative research
    • Sharing experiences and case studies
    • Asking questions and proposing diverse solutions to policy challenges
  • Guest expert sessions and mentorship: Bringing experienced economists and policy practitioners to mentor members in lectures and one-on-one sessions.
  • Case studies and workshops (policy labs): Applied learning through:
    • Team-based design or revision of new or existing policies
    • Data analysis for research-informed decisions
    • Simulations for foresight and scenario planning to understand policy impacts
  • Fully funded research quests and publications: Opportunities to pursue independent and collaborative research that culminate in CoP publications.

Engagement Model

How we work with stakeholders to move policies from paper into practice.

  • Facilitating dialogue sessions: Bringing together stakeholders and affected communities to co-create policy interventions for developmental impact.
  • Inclusive youth-led collaborations: Partnering with governments, NGOs, and communities to align our policies with real-world needs and solutions.
  • Information and communication: Turning findings into action by publishing accessible materials (magazines, journals, reports) to inform and engage the broader community.
  • Campaign programs: Organizing symposiums, conferences, and workshops that advocate for policy reforms and highlight effective policy practices that tackle developmental challenges.
